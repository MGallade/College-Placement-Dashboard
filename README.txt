COLLEGE PLACEMENT ANALYSIS

Overview
--------
This project analyzes factors associated with entry-level placement salaries among college students.
The objective is to understand how demographics, skill readiness, and job-preparedness indicators
relate to median placement outcomes.

Dataset
-------
- Dataset Size: ~100,000 student records
- Nature: Simulated / anonymized academic placement data
- Key Variables:
  - Academic performance (10th %, 12th %, degree %)
  - Skill indices (technical, soft skills, aptitude, communication)
  - Job readiness indicators (internships, projects, leadership roles, backlogs)
  - Placement salary (median-based analysis)

Methodology
-----------
- Skill indices were constructed as normalized composite scores derived from multiple assessment
  parameters and scaled to a 0–10 range.
- Analysis primarily uses median salary comparisons to reduce the impact of outliers.
- Visual analytics were used to identify patterns and associations across variables.

Key Insights
------------
- Demographic and academic factors show limited association with entry-level salary variation.
- Skill readiness indicators demonstrate a strong and consistent relationship with median salaries.
- Internships and job-relevant projects are positively associated with higher entry-level pay.
- Higher backlog counts are associated with lower median placement salaries, indicating potential
  employability concerns.

Disclaimer
----------
The relationships presented in this analysis are associative and do not imply causation.
The findings are intended to highlight observable trends rather than establish causal effects.
